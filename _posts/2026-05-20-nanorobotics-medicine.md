## What Are Nanorobots?

**Scale:** 1-1000 nanometers. A human hair is 80,000 nanometers wide.
**Materials:** DNA origami, magnetic nanoparticles, biodegradable polymers, carbon nanotubes.
**Power:** External magnetic fields, chemical reactions, or biological propulsion.
**Control:** Magnetic steering, ultrasound guidance, or autonomous chemical sensing.

## Where We Are in 2026

### Tumor Targeting (Most Advanced)
**Status:** Phase I/II clinical trials
**Results:** 90% reduction in drug side effects, 3x more drug reaches the tumor
**Companies:** Nanobiotix (France), BIND Therapeutics (US), Selecta Biosciences (US)
**Timeline:** FDA approval expected 2028-2030

### Blood Clot Removal
**Status:** Pre-clinical (animal trials complete)
**Results:** Complete clot dissolution in 95% of animal cases, no vessel damage
**Implications:** Stroke treatment could go from "golden hour" to "golden minutes"

### Targeted Drug Delivery
**Status:** Phase I trials
**Applications:** Crohn's disease (drugs only in inflamed sections), arthritis (direct to joints), brain tumors (cross blood-brain barrier)

### Dental Nanobots
**Status:** Early clinical trials (surprisingly advanced!)
**Results:** 95% plaque removal in lab studies
**Timeline:** Consumer product possible by 2028

## The Big Challenges

### 1. Immune System Response
Your body destroys foreign invaders. Nanorobots trigger immune responses.
**Solution:** PEG coating, biodegradable materials, immune-suppressing surfaces.

### 2. Precise Navigation
Steering a 100nm robot through 60,000 miles of blood vessels is hard.
**Current approach:** External magnetic fields + chemical gradients + AI navigation.

### 3. Safety and Regulation
What happens when nanorobots accumulate in your liver?
**Status:** FDA Nanotechnology Task Force created. First approvals expected 2028-2030.

### 4. Manufacturing at Scale
Building billions of identical nanorobots is a massive engineering challenge.
**Current yield:** ~70% functional. **Needed:** 99.9%+ for clinical use.
