## The Foundation: What Actually Extends Lifespan

Before you buy a single supplement, get these right. They account for 80% of longevity outcomes.

### 1. Sleep (Non-Negotiable)

**The data:** Every study on longevity shows sleep as the #1 predictor. Less than 6 hours increases all-cause mortality by 13%. More than 9 hours is equally bad.

**What works:**
- 7-8 hours, consistent schedule
- Room temperature: 65-68°F (18-20°C)
- No screens 1 hour before bed (or use blue-light blockers)
- Magnesium glycinate (400mg) before bed

### 2. Zone 2 Cardio (The Underrated King)

**The data:** 150+ minutes/week of Zone 2 cardio reduces cardiovascular mortality by 40% and all-cause mortality by 25%. Nothing else comes close.

**What works:**
- 30-45 minutes, 4-5x/week
- Heart rate: 60-70% of max (can hold a conversation)
- Activities: brisk walking, cycling, swimming, rowing

### 3. Resistance Training (Muscle = Longevity)

**The data:** Muscle mass is the strongest predictor of survival in older adults. Sarcopenia (muscle loss) starts at 30 and accelerates at 50.

**What works:**
- 3-4x/week, compound movements
- Progressive overload (add weight/reps over time)
- Protein: 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight
- Creatine monohydrate: 5g/day

## The Tier 2 Stack: Evidence-Based Supplements

### NMN / NR (NAD+ Precursors)
**Dose:** NMN 500-1000mg/day OR NR 300-600mg/day
**Effect:** Boosts NAD+, a molecule critical for cellular energy that declines 50% by age 50.

### Creatine Monohydrate
**Dose:** 5g/day, every day, forever.
**Effect:** Improves muscle performance, brain function, and cellular hydration. 500+ studies.

### Vitamin D3 + K2
**Dose:** Vitamin D3 4000-5000 IU/day + K2 (MK-7) 200mcg/day
**Effect:** 42% of Americans are deficient. Supplementation reduces all-cause mortality by 6%.

### Magnesium (Glycinate or Threonate)
**Dose:** 400mg glycinate before bed OR 2000mg threonate for cognitive benefits
**Effect:** Involved in 600+ enzymatic reactions. Most people are deficient.
